1. Derivatives

1.1. Chain Rule

The chain rule is a formula the expresses the derivative of the composition of two functions \(f\) and \(g\) in terms of the derivatives of \(f\) and \(g\). Therefore if we want to find the derivative of \(h(x) = f(g(x))\) then

\[

\begin{array}{l} h'(x) = f'(g(x))g'(x) \\ \text{or equivalently} \\ h' = f(h \circ g)' = (f' \circ g)\cdot g' \\ \end{array}

\]

1.2. Product Rule

The product rule (also known as the Leibniz rule or Leibniz product rule) is a formula used to find the derivative of products of two or more functions.

\[

\begin{array}{l} (uv)' = u'*v + u*v' \\ \text{or in Leibniz's notation} \\ \frac{d(uv)}{dx} = \frac{du}{dz}v + u \frac{dv}{dx} \\ \end{array}

\]
