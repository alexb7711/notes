#+title: The Cauchy Schwartz Inequality

#+begin_theorem
In an inner product space $S$ with the induced norm $||\cdot ||$ we have the following inequality

$$
\big< x, y \big> \le ||x||^2 ||y||^2
$$
#+end_theorem

* Proof
Let $x$ and $y$ be any two vectors in $S$ and let $\alpha \in \mathbb{R}$ where $\alpha = \frac{< x,y >}{||y||^2}$.

\begin{equation*}
\begin{array}{rll}
0 & \le ||x - \alpha y||^2                 &\\
  & = < x,x > - 2\alpha < x,y > + \alpha^2 < y,y > &\\
  & = < x,x > - 2\frac{< x,y >}{||y||^2}< x,y > + \frac{< x,y >^2}{||y||^4} < y,y > & \text{Substitute $\alpha$ in} \\
0 & \le ||x||^2 - 2\frac{< x,y >^2}{||y||^2} + \frac{< x,y >^2}{||y||^2} & \text{simplify with } < y,y > = ||y||^2 \\
0 & \le ||x||^2 - \frac{< x,y >^2}{||y||^2} & \\
||x||^2 & \ge \frac{< x,y >^2}{||y||^2} \\
< x,y >^2 & \le ||x||^2 ||y||^2
\end{array}
\end{equation*}

* Cauchy Schwartz for functions
Similarly we can write

$$
\Big( \int_a^b f(t)g(t) dt\Big)^2 \le int_a^b f^2(t) dt \int_a^b g(t) dt
$$

* Cauchy Schwartz in equality
With the Cauchy Schwartz inequality we can consider $< x,y >$ as a measure of some relation between $x$ and $y$. When do we have equality?

For real values we have equality when $x$ is a scalar of $y$ ($x = \alpha y$)! This is true for complex numbers when $x = \alpha y$ for $\alpha \in \mathbb{C}$, in other words, $x$ and $y$ are linearly dependent.
