** Two-Stage Problem
:PROPERTIES:
:CUSTOM_ID: two-stage-problem
:END:
The idea of two-stage stochastic programming is that (optimal) decisions
should be based on data at the time the decisions are made and cannot
depend on future observations. The two-stag formulation is widely used
in stochastic programming. The general formulation of a two-stage
stochastic programming problem is [[#.md][1]] :

$$

min_{x \in X}{g(x) = f(x) + E_{[[file:\zeta]]}[[file:Q(x,\zeta)]]}

$$

where \(A(x,\zeta)\) is the optimal value of the second-stage problem

\[
min_{y}\{q(y,\zeta) | T(\zeta)x + W(\zeta)y = h(\zeta)\} \\
\]
