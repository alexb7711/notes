2. Introduction

  • The most widely-used traditional strategy is the constant-current constant-voltage (CC-CV) [9] strategy, in which a constant current is applied to the battery until the terminal voltage reaches a specified value, and afterward the charging current decreases by applying a constant voltage to the terminals of the battery.

-In this paper we look at the charging problem from a fresh perspective using optimal control theory, and our goal is to find the optimal current profile that minimizes a specific cost function.

3. Analytic Solution for Optimal Charging Current Profile

  • The model consists of a voltage source corresponding to the open-circuit voltage (OCV), which is dependent on the state of charge (SOC), and a resistance \(R_0\).
  • The OCV is a nonlinear function of SOC and is denoted by \(V_0(s[k])\).
  • The SOC battery dynamics is written as
\begin{equation} \begin{array}{c} i[k] = \frac{1}{R_0}(v_c - V_0(s[k])) \\ s[k+1] = s[k] + c_hi[k] \\ c_h = \frac{\Delta}{3600C_{\text{batt}}} \end{array} \end{equation}

where \(C_{\text{batt}}\) is assumed the battery capacity in Ah, \(v_c\) is the voltage of the battery at maximum SOC, and \(V_0\) is the initial voltage at an SOC of \(s[k]\).
